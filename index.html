Pro Tips

  • Get practice in low-risk situations.
  • Put together session proposals for local events.
  • Put your code on github (or drupal.org).
  • Take on freelance or side projects.
  • Work with your employer to develop a career path.

This means that given a field instance of field_reference1, if you add a reference to NodeBeta to NodeAlpha's field_reference1 and NodeBeta has an instance of field_reference1, then NodeAlpha will be added to NodeBeta's instance of field_reference1.

Be a mentor.

What do mentors do?

  • Review code/development with individuals one-on-one.
  • Listen to presentations and giving feedback.
  • Make it public that you can offer your time to people who are struggling.
  • Give career advice and consultation.
[image here]

Don’t think you’re capable of being a mentor? Think again.

Speak.

Community Benefits

  • Helps disseminate knowledge
  • Encourages other women to speak up

Personal Benefits

  • Helps you understand your material better.
  • Gives you greater recognition in community and in workplace.
  • Makes your company look good.
  • Reaffirms you’re not an impostor. You have a valuable voice!

Own your career.

Take credit for accomplishments
(& keep a record of them).

Don’t say:

  • "I just Googled it."
  • "It's no big deal."
  • "She did most of the work."

Say instead:

  • Thank you!

Decide what you want,
and ask for it.

If you want to move up at your current job...

Pro Tips

  • Communicate with your boss about the skills you want to grow.
  • Know the responsibilities (and job description) of the role above yours.
  • Build a case for promotions based on your work.

But don't overwork yourself.

If you want another job...

“Here’s where I think women get it wrong: we are perpetually rounding down, where, by all rules of mathematics, we should be rounding up. And that slight miscalculation has huge repercussions in our professional lives.”

https://hbr.org/2012/01/confidence-is-a-numbers-game/

Hewlett Packard statistics:

  • Women wouldn't apply for a job unless they feel they meet 100% of the qualifications.
  • Men applied if they felt they meet as little as 60% of them.

Remember: You do not have a moral obligation to tell prospective employers about the limits of your knowledge.

Spend your time demonstrating what you know, not disclaiming what you don't.
